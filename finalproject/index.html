Brief Informational

Maine Coons are a cat breed that draws alot of attention and popularity from their naturally large stature relative to other cats. They are sized much much larger than the average cat, although not all Maine Coons grow to be insanely large. Still they are often the perfect choice for a cat owner who wants a large pet to play and cuddle with.

They are a long haired cat, very identifiable by their pointy ears and unique facial structure that you can notice in the given pictures (right). They are usually very desirable purebred and as such can sometimes be quite expensive. They also being so large require more food than some cats and unique ammenities such as larger litterboxes, prospective owners should be prepared for some additional expenses.

Fun Facts

  • Maine Coon Cats actually love to swim in water, much unlike most cats who hate water. This is due to sleek fur that lets them move easily in water

  • Maine Coon cats have been domesticated from larger wild cats over a very long time, but they still retain much of their hunters instinct.

  • Maine Coon's may retain a hunter instinct but they are actually referred to often as 'Gentle Giants' due to their playful and friendly nature

  • Maine Coon cats used to often have 6 toes
